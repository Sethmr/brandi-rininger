Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about buying, selling, and living in Western North Carolina's Carolina Smokies region.

What areas does Brandi Rininger serve in Western North Carolina? ▾
Service Area

I serve 17 communities across the Carolina Smokies region including Franklin, Bryson City, Sylva, Highlands, Cashiers, Cherokee, Murphy, Robbinsville, Asheville, Waynesville, Hendersonville, Weaverville, Black Mountain, Brevard, Burnsville, Spruce Pine, and Mars Hill. My home base is Franklin in Macon County.

Does Brandi help buyers relocating from Florida or other states? ▾
Relocation

Yes! Relocation is one of my specialties. I help many buyers moving from Florida, Georgia, and other southern states to Western North Carolina. I can guide you through everything from understanding mountain climate differences to finding the right community, navigating well and septic systems, and connecting you with local contractors and services. Check out my Florida relocation guide for more details.

What types of properties does Brandi specialize in? ▾
Services

I specialize in mountain homes, land and acreage, cabins and second homes, off-grid and homesteading properties, and investment properties including short-term rental cabins. I also help first-time homebuyers navigate the process. Whether you're looking for a family home in Franklin or 50 acres of unrestricted mountain land, I can help.

Can Brandi help me find land or acreage in Western NC? ▾
Land

Absolutely! Land is one of my primary specialties. I help buyers find everything from riverfront lots to large unrestricted acreage across Macon County and beyond. I understand the unique considerations for mountain land — things like road access, water sources, topography, soil percolation for septic, and zoning restrictions. See my guide to buying land in WNC for a full breakdown.

How much does it cost to buy a home in the Franklin NC area? ▾
Pricing

The Franklin and Western NC market ranges from around $65,000 for land parcels up to $2 million+ for luxury mountain estates. The median home price varies by community — Franklin tends to be more affordable than areas like Highlands or Asheville. I provide free comparative market analyses so you know exactly what to expect in your target area. Read my WNC land price guide for current data by county.

What should I know about buying off-grid property in Western NC? ▾
Land

Off-grid properties in WNC require careful evaluation of water sources (springs, wells, creeks), road access and maintenance agreements, solar exposure for energy, soil conditions for septic, cell service availability, and county regulations. I have experience with off-grid and homesteading properties and can help you find the right fit. Check out my Off-Grid & Homesteading Guide for detailed information.

What makes Franklin NC a good place to live? ▾
Area Info

Franklin offers an incredible quality of life — low cost of living compared to Asheville and other mountain towns, a charming small-town downtown, proximity to the Nantahala National Forest and Great Smoky Mountains, four mild seasons, a growing arts and food scene, and a welcoming community. It's especially popular with retirees and families relocating from Florida and other southern states. Read more in my Franklin neighborhood guide.
