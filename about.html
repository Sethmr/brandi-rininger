About Brandi

Brandi Rininger is a licensed real estate broker with eXp Realty and the Rookie of the Year from the Carolina Smokies Association of Realtors, proudly serving homebuyers, sellers, and land investors across the Carolina Smokies region. With 50+ closed transactions and growing, Brandi helps clients find their perfect place in the mountains — from the charming streets of Franklin and Bryson City to the vibrant culture of Asheville.

Brandi got into real estate in Western North Carolina because she's always loved homes — but more importantly, she loves helping people through big life moments. Before becoming a broker, she was focused on her family and figuring out what she truly wanted long-term. When she and her family moved to the mountains from Florida about five years ago, something just clicked. They were drawn to the slower pace, the mountain views, and the sense of community. There's something about waking up to the mountains and being surrounded by nature that never gets old — it felt like home almost instantly. That's when Brandi knew she wanted to be the one helping others find that same feeling.

She loves seeing people fall in love with the mountains the same way her family did. Whether it's their first time visiting or they've been dreaming about it for years, being part of that moment is really special. The most rewarding part is helping someone find not just a house, but a place where they can build memories, slow down, and truly enjoy life.

Local Knowledge

Brandi primarily works in Franklin and throughout Macon County, but she's very familiar with the surrounding areas like Sylva, Bryson City, Otto, and beyond. She spends a lot of time exploring these areas, so she can help clients find the right fit — whether they want something private, close to town, or tucked away in the mountains. She also stays on top of what's coming up and what areas are gaining interest.

When it comes to local favorites, Brandi always recommends checking out Currahee Brewing Company for a relaxed vibe right on the river, Culture 828 for great pizza, and Just Donuts — a must, but you have to get there early before they sell out. She loves sharing little local spots like these with clients because it helps them feel connected to the area right away. Outside of work, she's always outdoors — whether it's going for a run, hiking, or just exploring new spots with her family. There are so many beautiful trails and waterfalls around here, and even a simple drive through the mountains can be incredible. Fall is one of her favorite times with all the colors, but every season has something special.
